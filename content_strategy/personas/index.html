Personas

Overview

Two UX colleagues and I were asked to create user personas for Isilon. These personas would be used as tools to help align business requirements with customer wants and needs.

A persona is a fictional character that represents a customer or target audience. A persona has a name, a picture, some demographic information, and other meaningful characteristics. Personas are based on user research—in this case, the Isilon personas are based on customer interviews, site visits, and surveys—and are composites of real people from the group the persona represents.

The Isilon user personas that we created represent new and veteran System Administrators working in the health sciences, media and entertainment, and private sector verticals. Each persona includes a quote from a real user, a short bio, a list of goals and motivations, daily tasks, and pain points. These personas are used by engineering, documentation, and training teams for planning and developing user-centered product requirements.
